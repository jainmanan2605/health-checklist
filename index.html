Connect your GitHub account

Your check-ins are stored in a private GitHub Gist in your account, so they sync across all your devices. One-time setup per device:

  1. Open github.com/settings/tokens/new (pre-filled for you)
  2. Set expiration to No expiration (or your preference)
  3. Make sure only the gist scope is ticked
  4. Click Generate token and copy it

The token is kept only in this browser and is used solely to read/write one private gist. It cannot touch your repositories.

✨ All done — great job!

Your habits

Drag the ⠿ handle to reorder habits — the order is saved and used on the Today screen too. Rename habits by typing directly in the box. Each habit can be daily, a weekly target (e.g. 3×/week), or tied to specific weekdays. Changing a habit's frequency only applies from today forward — past months and years keep the schedule they were actually measured against. Deleting a habit keeps its past history in old reports.

Backup & safety

Your data lives in a private GitHub Gist in your own account and is cached on this device, so it is safe and syncs everywhere. For extra peace of mind you can keep an offline copy:

A backup is a single JSON file with every habit and check-in. Restoring merges it with your current data (newer wins), then re-syncs to GitHub.
